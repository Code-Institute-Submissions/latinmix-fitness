Salsa

Salsa is a Latin dance associated with the music genre of the same name which originated in the 1960s in New York City. It was primarily developed by Dominicans and Puerto Ricans living in New York in the late 1960s and early 1970s.
In many styles of salsa dancing, as a dancer shifts their weight by stepping into the middle to have 50/50 weight, the upper body remains level and nearly unaffected by the weight changes. The basic Salsa dance rhythm consists of taking three steps for every four beats of music.
